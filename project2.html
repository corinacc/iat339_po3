Pavilion Design for Holland Park

About the Project

This project is created from IAT 233, a course focused on spatial design. This project is to build a functional and aesthetic Pavilion for Holland Park in 3d modelling. As a team, we review the location sketches from our field visit to Holland Park and decide on one site or location for our pavilion

Skills Gained

  • Sketches & hand-making
  • Spatial thinking and design
  • 3D model software (rhino)

Location Select & Form Gerneration

The main function of this Pavilion is art gallery. This location is near the center of the park where it is the most popular area, thus it naturally attracts visitors to visit the gallery. The reason of placing the pavilion at this specific location is easy for visitor.

Process

I take our findings about annotations and sketches, and determine a final location for our pavilion in Holland Park. Following this, we completed a two-dimensional design prototype using geometric shapes cut in fabric to compose possible forms for the pavilion.

We use the element and principles of Symmetry, Axis, Datum and Hierarchy by shape and size. Then, We use Rhino software to build this 3D model of Pavilion with potential and different materials.

The Pergola: From the initial sketch to the final design, multiple small adjustments had to be made to better afford the function of the building and or polishing the overall structure. While the general shape of the building was maintained throughout the process, the shape of the fences was adjusted from the Rhino model to the one in the final submission to create a more dynamic and organic feel.
